## Growth: First-Year Students Age 25+ Growing +19.7% Annually

**Growth Rates (First-Year Student Cohort)**

| Demographic | Growth Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **First-year students age 25+** | **+19.7%** | VERIFIED |
| First-year students age 21-24 | +16.7% | Related cohort |
| Traditional first-year students | +3.4% | For comparison |

**Key Insight:** First-year enrollment of older adults (25+) is growing nearly **6x faster** than traditional-age first-year students.

**Verified Sources:**

## Why Now: Demographic Pressure

**The Enrollment Cliff**

| Projection | Count | Details |
|---|---|---|
| HS graduate decline | -13% | 2023-2041 |
| Timeline to full impact | 2041 | ~15 years |
| Regional variation | -17% to -20% | Northeast, West most affected |
| Rebound regions | +3% | South only |

**Impact:** Traditional college-age recruitment becomes increasingly competitive. Non-traditional (25+) students represent a growing, underserved market.

**Verified Source:**
- WICHE (Western Interstate Commission for Higher Education)
- "Knocking at the College Door" (11th edition, 2024)
- https://findingequilibriumfutureshighered.substack.com/p/a-look-at-the-enrollment-cliff
