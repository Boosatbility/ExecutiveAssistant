## TAM: US Higher Education Market (Total Addressable Market)

## SAM: Non-Traditional Student Market (Serviceable Addressable Market)

**2023 Non-Traditional Market Size**

| Metric | Count | % of TAM |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Non-traditional students | 12M | 74% |
| Institutions serving non-traditional | ~3,500-3,700 | 91% |

**NCES Official Definition (7 characteristics - any one qualifies):**
1. Delayed enrollment (>1 year after high school)
2. Attended part-time
3. Worked full-time (35+ hours/week) while enrolled
4. Financially independent
5. Had dependents (other than spouse)
6. Single parent
7. Earned GED/certificate instead of diploma

**Non-Traditional Student Breakdown (overlapping characteristics):**

| Characteristic | Count | % of All Students |
|---|---|---|
| Age 25+ | 3.9M | 24% |
| First-generation | 7.5M | 46% |
| Working (while enrolled) | 10.4M | 64% |
| - Full-time work | 6.5M | 40% |
| - Part-time work | 3.9M | 24% |
| Financially independent | 8.0M | 49% |
| Have dependents | 3.9M | 24% |
| **At least one non-traditional trait** | ~12M | 74% |

**SAM Definition:** Non-traditional student populations (74% of all undergraduates across 91% of institutions offering online/flexible delivery).

**Key Insight:** Non-traditional students represent the majority of the market but institutions lack visibility into their struggles. This is Boostability's addressable market.

## Boostability Positioning for Morgan Stanley

- **TAM:** 18.3M students across 4,000 institutions
- **SAM:** 12M non-traditional students across ~3,500-3,700 institutions
- **Opportunity:** Non-traditional students are under-served by traditional student success tools. Institutions lack visibility into their specific struggles (assignment clarity, time management, career relevance).
- **Boostability's Angle:** AI-powered platform designed for non-traditional learner realities: working students, part-time attendance, financial pressure, family responsibilities.
