# High School Graduate Projections (2023-2041)

## National Projection

### Year-by-Year Decline

| Year | HS Graduates | % Change from Peak (2025) | % Change from 2023 |
|------|--------------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| 2025 | 3.8-3.9M | **Peak** | — |
| 2030 | ~3.77M | -3.1% | -3.1% |
| 2041 | 3.4M | -13% | -13% |

### Summary

| Metric | Data | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **Peak Year** | **2025** | Highest HS grad count in history |
| **2030 Decline** | **-3.1%** | Near-term impact (6 years) |
| **2041 Decline** | **-13%** | Long-term structural shift |
| **Total Decline (Peak to 2041)** | **~500,000 fewer graduates** | Cumulative loss |

### Decline Pattern

- Decline is "slower and steadier" (not cliff-like)
- Driven by: low birth rates during 2007-2009 Great Recession
- Sharpest decline period: 2025-2029
- Long-term pressure: continues through 2041

## Why This Matters for Higher Ed

**The Enrollment Cliff Impact:**

1. **Immediate pressure (2026-2030):** Institutions compete harder for declining traditional student pool
2. **Demographic necessity:** Non-traditional (25+) students become strategic focus, not optional
3. **Geographic variation:** Institutions in West/Northeast face existential pressure; South has breathing room
4. **Regional winners/losers:** Schools that serve non-traditional will thrive; those dependent on HS grads will struggle

**For Boostability:**
- Institutions face urgent need to serve non-traditional students
- This need is NOT evenly distributed (West/Northeast face immediate crisis)
- Non-traditional student support is now a competitive necessity, not a nice-to-have

---

## Root Cause: Birth Rate Decline (Great Recession)

**Timeline:**
- **2007-2009:** Great Recession → fewer births
- **2025-2026:** First cohort with low birth rates reaches college age
- **2025-2029:** Sharpest enrollment cliff impact
- **2030-2041:** Ongoing pressure from continued low birth rates

**Recovery prospects:** No major recovery projected before 2041. Long-term structural shift.
