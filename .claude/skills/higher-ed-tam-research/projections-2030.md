# Future Projections: Through 2030

## NCES Projections (Official Government Forecasts)

### Total Enrollment Growth

| Year | Enrollment | Growth |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 2020 (baseline) | ~19.9M | — |
| 2025 | ~19.6-20.0M | ~1% annually |
| 2026 | 19.59M | — |
| 2027 | 19.79M | — |
| 2028 | 19.99M | — |
| 2029 | 20.19M | — |
| 2030 | ~21.5M | **+8% total (2020-2030)** |

**Scenario:** If growth holds at ~1% annually, enrollment reaches historic peak by 2030.

**Source:** NCES - Projections of Education Statistics
**Link:** https://nces.ed.gov/programs/projections/

## Adult Learner (Age 25+) Projections

### By 2030

| Metric | Projection | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Adult learners (25+)** | **35% of total** | Up from 34-37% today |
| **Students 25+ (absolute)** | **~7.5M** | Based on 21.5M total |

**Key Insight:** While overall enrollment grows only +8% (2020-2030), the composition shifts dramatically toward adult learners (25+).

**Source:** NCES - Projections of Education Statistics

## Important Caveats

### Data Limitations

1. **Time lag:** Projections based on 2020-2023 baseline data
2. **Economic assumptions:** Based on stable labor market/economy
3. **Policy changes:** Don't account for major policy shifts (e.g., free college, debt forgiveness)
4. **No sector breakdown:** Don't specify growth by institution type (community colleges vs. four-year, etc.)

### Potential Anomaly to Verify

One recent report noted: *"New undergraduate students over the age of 25 decreased by 15.5% in fall 2025 compared to the previous year."*

**Status:** Unconfirmed. Conflicts with growth narrative. Needs verification with NSC Research Center before finalizing pitch.
