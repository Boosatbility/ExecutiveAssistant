# Why Now: Market Drivers

## Driver 1: Non-Traditional Growth Massively Outpaces Traditional

**Year-Over-Year Growth (Fall 2024)**

| Segment | Growth Rate |
|---------|------------|
| First-year students 25+ | **+19.7%** |
| First-year students 21-24 | **+16.7%** |
| Traditional first-year | +3.4% |

**Why This Matters:** Non-traditional is growing 5-6x faster than traditional students.

**Quote:**
*"In fall 2024, enrollment of first-year students ages 25 and older spiked by 19.7%. Older students largely drove the growth in first-year students, with enrollment of first-year students ages 21 to 24 surging 16.7% in fall 2024."*

**Source:** National Student Clearinghouse Research Center / Higher Ed Dive
**Link:** https://www.highereddive.com/news/fall-2024-final-college-enrollment-clearinghouse/738054/

**Pitch Bullet:**
- First-year students 25+ grew 19.7% in fall 2024 — while traditional college-age enrollment stagnates

---

## Driver 2: Traditional Student Decline Creates Urgency

**High School Graduate Projections (2023-2041)**

| Region | Decline |
|--------|---------|
| **National** | **-13%** |
| Northeast | -17% |
| Midwest | -16% |
| West | -20% |
| South | +3% |

**Why This Matters:** Institutions must replace lost traditional students. Non-traditional students are the only growth lever available.

**Quote:**
*"The Western Interstate Commission for Higher Education (WICHE) projects a 13% decline in the total number of U.S. high school graduates from 2023 to 2041, peaking in 2025 before steadily falling, often called the 'enrollment cliff.'"*

**Source:** WICHE - "Knocking at the College Door" (11th edition, 2024)
**Link:** https://findingequilibriumfutureshighered.substack.com/p/a-look-at-the-enrollment-cliff

**Pitch Bullet:**
- 13% decline in HS graduates through 2041 forces institutions to recruit differently

---

## Driver 3: Adult Learners Now THE Growth Engine

**Market Shift**

- Adult learners no longer "niche" — they're 34-37% of total enrollment TODAY
- Projected to be 35% by 2030
- Growing at 16.7-19.7% YoY while traditional declines
- Only segment offsetting demographic headwinds

**Quote:**
*"Adult learners are no longer a 'niche' market—they are the market, and adult learners themselves are shifting to center stage as the key drivers of growth for undergraduate enrollment."*

**Source:** EAB (Education Advisory Board)
**Link:** https://eab.com/resources/blog/adult-education-blog/what-2024-taught-us-about-the-future-of-graduate-and-online-education/

**Alternative Quote:**
*"Growth in overall enrollment appears to be sustained partly by increased enrollment from adult learners and other non-traditional student populations offsetting declines in traditional college-age students."*

**Source:** NCES Projections of Education Statistics
**Link:** https://nces.ed.gov/programs/projections/

**Pitch Bullet:**
- Adult learners (25+) now the growth engine — institutions that don't serve them will lose market share

---

## Combined "Why Now" Narrative

1. **Traditional students declining** (-13% HS grads by 2041)
2. **Non-traditional students surging** (+19.7% first-year 25+ in fall 2024)
3. **Institutions have no choice** — must pivot to serve non-traditional to survive

**Market Opportunity:** 5.9-6.3M underserved non-traditional students across 3,500-3,700 institutions that lack specialized tools and insights for this demographic.
