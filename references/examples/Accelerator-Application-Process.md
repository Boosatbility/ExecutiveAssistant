# Accelerator Application Process & Skills
**Boostability | Developed March 2026**

## Overview
Systematic approach to completing accelerator applications for investor programs. Used successfully for Morgan Stanley MSISV (submitted March 31, 2026).

---

## Phase 1: Research & Strategy

### Program Analysis
- Identify program thesis (what founders/companies do they fund?)
- Research cohort profiles and founder demographics
- Note specific requirements (pitch deck format, Q&A character limits, financial statements, etc.)
- Clarify deadline and submission process

**MSISV Example:** Morgan Stanley focuses on underrepresented founders solving for underserved populations. Emphasis on founder-market fit + measurable social impact.

### Founder-Program Fit Assessment
- Does your founder story align with program values?
- Are your target customers/users aligned with program focus?
- Do you have early traction to show?

**MSISV Example:** Efrat's ADHD + lived experience = founder-market fit. First-generation/minority/neurodivergent students = underserved populations. 50+ paying customers = real traction.

## Phase 2: Content Preparation

### Application Answers (Q&A)
**Process:**
1. Read all questions carefully
2. Note character limits for each (typically under 1000 chars)
3. Draft answers with full detail first
4. Trim to fit character limits while maintaining authentic voice
5. Remove all acronyms - spell out in full (LMS → learning management systems, SMS → text message, VP → Vice President)
6. Eliminate em-dashes (—), use regular hyphens (-)
7. Avoid corporate jargon - use natural, conversational tone

**Character Limit Strategy:**
- Don't aim for minimum (too thin)
- Aim for 750-900 chars to use full allowance while staying under limit
- Use critiques + multiple options to find strongest direction
- Prioritize authentic voice over polish

**Critical Answers for MSISV:**
- Problem Statement: Make explicit that you serve underrepresented/underserved populations
- Solution: Show how it uniquely addresses their needs
- Founders & Team: Lead with founder-market fit (lived experience, personal connection to problem)
- Program Priorities: Explain what the accelerator specifically unlocks (capital, credibility, networks)
- Competitors: Show founder-built differentiation, not just feature gaps
- Pivots: Show how you listened to users and adapted

### Pitch Deck
**Structure (Must-Have Slides):**
1. Cover/Title
2. Founder + Story (with lived experience emphasized)
3. Problem (explicit about underrepresented populations)
4. Solution/Product
5. Product Demo/Screenshots (if available)
6. Business Model
7. Market Opportunity (TAM/SAM/SOM with consistency)
8. Traction & Validation (real numbers, not projections)
9. Team
10. Why Now/Market Ready
11. Competitive Advantage
12. Go-To-Market Strategy
13. Financial Projections
14. Funding Ask + Use of Funds
15. Closing/Vision

**MSISV-Specific Slides:**
- Emphasize founder-market fit on page 2 (lived ADHD experience)
- Make underrepresented populations explicit on problem slide
- Show real traction (not just pilots)
- Include impact metrics (student outcomes: pass rates, persistence, dropout prevention)
- Consider framing as ESG investment (social impact + measurement)

**Design Standards:**
- Professional but not over-designed
- Consistent branding and logo placement
- Real product screenshots > mockups
- Clear captions for all visuals
- Data-backed numbers with sources

### Financial Statements
**What to prepare:**
- P&L Statement (2 years if available)
- Monthly expense breakdown
- Burn rate analysis
- Revenue projections (if pre-revenue, be clear about it)
- Cap table (equity structure)

**Format:**
- CSV for data tables (easy to update)
- Include monthly trends to show growth/seasonal patterns
- Annotate high expenses (e.g., "November spike due to international travel")
- Be transparent about stage (pre-revenue, pilot stage, early revenue)

**MSISV Note:** They want clarity on current burn vs. revenue. Frame conservatively if early stage.

### Cap Table
- Clear ownership structure
- Vesting schedules if applicable
- Options/grants with conversion terms
- Export to XLS for easy sharing

## Phase 3: Deck Refinement (Specific to MSISV)

### Critical Fixes Checklist
- [ ] Founder story includes personal ADHD/lived experience detail
- [ ] Underrepresented populations named explicitly (first-gen, minority, neurodivergent, non-traditional)
- [ ] Student numbers verified and consistent across deck + application
- [ ] Market sizing (TAM/SAM) matches across all materials
- [ ] Traction clarity (what's current vs. projected?)
- [ ] Product screenshots included (Add Activity, Dashboard, SMS/SMS Nudging)
- [ ] Faculty/360 dashboard shown with real use case
- [ ] No acronyms (spell out all)
- [ ] No em-dashes (use regular hyphens)
- [ ] Impact metrics visible (pass rates, retention, dropout prevention)
- [ ] ESG/mission framing considered

### Feedback & Iteration Process
1. Get external feedback on deck (ideally from someone familiar with investor expectations)
2. Critique feedback before implementing (don't make changes just because someone suggested it)
3. Prioritize fixes that align with program thesis (for MSISV: founder-market fit + impact for underserved)
4. Skip "nice-to-have" slides if deadline is tight
5. Lock in final version 24 hours before deadline

---

## Phase 4: Quality Assurance

### Pre-Submission Checklist
- [ ] All Q&A answers under character limits
- [ ] No acronyms anywhere (search for common ones: LMS, SMS, VP, AI, etc.)
- [ ] Character counts verified (not estimated)
- [ ] Numbers consistent across application + deck (student counts, revenue, TAM/SAM)
- [ ] Tone consistent (authentic, not corporate)
- [ ] Founder story prominent and personal
- [ ] Underrepresented angle explicit
- [ ] Impact metrics visible
- [ ] Deck exported to PDF
- [ ] All files named clearly with date

### Final Review (24 hours before)
- Read application answers aloud to catch awkward phrasing
- Check deck one more time for typos/consistency
- Verify all contact info is correct
- Confirm submission process and deadline time zone

## Key Lessons Learned

### What Works
✅ **Founder-market fit first** - Personal connection to problem resonates more than polished pitch
✅ **Real traction over projections** - Actual paying customers > theoretical market size
✅ **Authentic voice** - Conversational tone builds trust; corporate speak feels generic
✅ **Explicit about values** - Name underrepresented populations, measurement, impact clearly
✅ **Visual proof** - Real product screenshots > mockups
✅ **Consistency** - Same numbers/story across deck + application
✅ **Transparency** - Be clear about stage (pre-revenue, early revenue, burn rate)

### What to Avoid
❌ Generic "struggling students" language - name who specifically
❌ Over-designed decks - clarity > beauty
❌ Acronyms - spell everything out (LMS, SMS, VP, etc.)
❌ Em-dashes (—) - use regular hyphens
❌ Mismatched numbers - verify consistency before submitting
❌ Burying differentiators - make founder-market fit obvious on page 2
❌ Rushing final review - quality check 24 hours before deadline
