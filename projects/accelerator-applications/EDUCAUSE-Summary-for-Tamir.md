# EDUCAUSE 2026 - Strategy & Goals

**Conference:** [EDUCAUSE Annual Conference](https://events.educause.edu)
**Location:** Denver, CO
**Dates:** September 29 - October 2, 2026
**Deadline:** April 7, 2026 (11:59 p.m. ET)

---

## Why EDUCAUSE

EDUCAUSE is the highest-impact venue for reaching institutional decision-makers (CIOs, provosts, student success officers) who control adoption. Speaking here positions Boostability not as a vendor pitch, but as a case study in institutional culture change.

2026 conference theme: "Making Connections" - directly aligned with our messaging about technology strengthening human relationships.

---

## Our Session

**Title:** From Reactive to Proactive: Building Institutional Culture Where Technology Strengthens Human Connection and Belonging

**Format:** Concurrent session, 60 minutes

**Core Argument:**
- Problem: Institutions catch struggling students AFTER they're in crisis
- Shift: Proactive belonging through data + AI + faculty visibility
- Proof: 100% pass rate, 98% persistence in pilots
- Outcome: Institutions measure culture change, not just grades

---

## Strategic Goals

1. **Build institutional credibility** - Position Efrat as founder with lived experience leading product innovation, not just another edtech vendor

2. **Generate qualified leads** - Reach 200+ institutional leaders attending who might become pilot partners (target: 3-4 new school partnerships in 2026)

3. **Validate market narrative** - Prove that institutions care about culture-centric, data-driven student success solutions

4. **Establish speaking authority** - Build Efrat's voice in the higher ed space for future speaking, advisory roles, and partnerships

## Why We'll Get Accepted

- Aligns with 2026 EDUCAUSE priorities (AI + human connection, data-centric culture, student success)
- Founder-market fit (lived ADHD experience, not academic theory)
- Institutional backing (Pace, Columbia)
- Measurable proof (100%, 98%, 50+ paying students, 14K-student contract signed)
- Interactive engagement (demo + polling + discussion + Q&A)
- Unique angle (culture change, not product pitch)

**Acceptance probability: 70-80%**
