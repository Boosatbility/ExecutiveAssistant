## Session Description (for program, ~100-150 words)

Neurodivergent students don't lack ability. They lack structure, accountability, and support designed for how their brains work. This session explores how AI can build that structure without judgment, automation, or accessibility afterthought.

We'll share a real-world platform built by someone who navigates ADHD daily, raising children with ADHD and learning disabilities. The platform breaks overwhelming assignments into daily tasks, sends personalized accountability nudges, and gives disability services directors real-time visibility into struggling students.

Results from pilot studies: 100% pass rate, 98% persistence, zero dropouts—with neurodivergent students at the center, not the margin.

Learn how to evaluate and implement AI tools that serve neurodivergent students as the primary design audience, not an afterthought.

---

## Learning Outcomes

Participants will be able to:

1. **Understand neurodivergent learner needs** specifically around structure, executive function, and accountability
2. **Evaluate AI accessibility solutions** using neurodiversity-first criteria (not disability compliance checkboxes)
3. **Support neurodivergent students** through technology partnerships between student success and disability services
4. **Measure outcomes** that matter to neurodivergent learners: completion, confidence, persistence

## Session Content/Outline (5-10 min per segment)

**1. Who Are Neurodivergent College Students (5 min)**
- ADHD, autism spectrum, dyslexia, dyscalculia, processing disorders
- Often first-generation, underdiagnosed, or late-identified
- Drop out at higher rates; least likely to access formal support

**2. The Accessibility Gap (5 min)**
- Formal disability services help, but have waitlists and require self-advocacy
- Consumer productivity apps not designed for executive function barriers
- LMS platforms deliver content, not structure
- The real barrier: overwhelm, procrastination, shame

**3. What Neurodivergent Students Actually Need (5 min)**
- Daily structure (not vague goals)
- Accountability without judgment
- Support from people who understand the struggle
- Measurable small wins that rebuild confidence

**4. Platform Demo: Built by Someone Who Lives It (5 min)**
- Assignment upload → AI breakdown into daily doable tasks
- Personalized SMS nudges (meet them where they are)
- Progress visibility (celebrate daily wins)
- No shame, no "fix yourself" messaging

**5. Disability Services Integration (5 min)**
- Real-time visibility into struggling students (not waiting for crisis)
- Data on what interventions work for whom
- Collaboration between student success and disability services
- Legal/privacy considerations

**6. Case Study: Real Results (3 min)**
- 100+ student pilot across 5+ universities
- Neurodivergent + first-gen + minority students
- 100% pass rate, 98% persistence, zero dropouts
- Now live with paying institution

**7. Implementation & Next Steps (3 min)**
- How to start: pilot with disability services
- What to measure: outcomes that matter (not just usage)
- Vendor evaluation: neurodiversity-first questions

**8. Q&A (2 min)**
