## Proposal Information

**Session Title:** From Reactive to Proactive: Building Institutional Culture Where Technology Strengthens Human Connection and Belonging

**Session Type:** Concurrent Session / 60 minutes

**Presenter:** Efrat Segal, Founder & CEO, Boostability

---

## Session Description (for program, ~100-150 words)

Most campus technology waits for students to fail before stepping in. This session explores a different approach: what happens when institutions shift from reactive crisis intervention to proactive belonging?

We'll examine how AI can strengthen human relationships—not replace them. When faculty see at-risk students early, they intervene with context and care. When students get structure that meets their actual needs, they show up. When institutions measure what matters (retention, completion, equity), they build accountability around their values.

The result: measurable culture change. First-generation, minority, neurodivergent, and non-traditional students who historically drop out now persist—not because the institution fixed them, but because the institution finally built for them.

Learn how technology becomes the enabler of institutional mission, not a side project.

---

## Learning Outcomes

Participants will be able to:

1. **Shift institutional thinking:** From "how do we use AI?" to "how does AI serve our mission to support all students?"
2. **Design human-centered technology:** Where AI strengthens faculty-student relationships, not replaces them
3. **Measure institutional culture:** Using data as accountability for what institutions say they care about
4. **Build equitable systems:** Where struggling students drive design decisions, not accommodation afterthoughts

---

## Session Content/Outline (5-10 min per segment)

**1. The Culture Question: Reactive vs. Proactive (5 min)**
- Most institutions catch students AFTER they're in crisis
- Faculty want to help but lack visibility until it's too late
- Technology has enabled scale, but not belonging
- The shift: technology as servant of institutional values, not driver of them

**2. The Problem is Culture, Not Technology (5 min)**
- 1 in 3 college students struggle; 1 in 4 drop out
- First-generation, minority, neurodivergent, non-traditional students struggle most
- Why? They were never taught how to learn, then blamed for not knowing
- Current tech assumes competence; doesn't build it
- Real barrier: institutions designed for students who already know the system

**3. Human Connection Powered by Data (5 min)**
- What if faculty knew which students were struggling before midterm?
- What if institutions measured their actual impact on at-risk populations?
- Data as accountability: "We care about equity, and here's the proof we're delivering it"
- AI as enabler: gives humans the information they need to connect meaningfully

**4. Live Demo: How This Works (5 min)**
- Student side: assignments broken into daily doable tasks (not overwhelming)
- Faculty side: see who's at risk, where they're stuck, what helps (not a black box)
- Institutional side: data on retention, completion, equity by population
- Center point: technology serves relationships, not replaces them

**5. Culture Shift in Action (5 min)**
- Case study: 100+ student pilot, 5+ universities
- Neurodivergent students historically left behind now show 100% pass rate, 98% persistence
- What changed? Institution built for them, not at them
- Data proves it works; now institutions can measure their values

**6. Implementation: Where IT Leaders Come In (5 min)**
- Culture change starts with technology leadership asking the right questions
- Not "what can AI do?" but "what do our students actually need?"
- How to measure institutional progress on equity
- Building systems where data reflects mission, not just metrics

**7. Q&A (2 min)**

## Engagement Strategy

Interactive and human-centered:
- Poll: "What does your institution measure as success for struggling students?" (reveals alignment/misalignment)

- Small group discussion: "Where is your institution reactive vs. proactive?"
- Student quote sharing (pilot feedback on what made the difference)
- Q&A focused on culture change, not technology selection

## Relevance to EDUCAUSE 2026: "Making Connections"

**Human Connection at Core:** This session directly addresses EDUCAUSE's 2026 theme of making connections. Technology is the enabler, but the goal is stronger relationships between students, faculty, and institutions.

**Culture is Critical:** Aligns with EDUCAUSE's 2026 insight that "the right culture is critical." Technology without cultural alignment fails. Data without purpose is just metrics.

**AI Literacy & Agency:** Moves beyond "deploying AI" to "creating a culture where AI serves learning, equity, and human connection."

**Data as Strategic Asset:** Shows how institutions use data not for surveillance, but for accountability to their stated values.

**Student-Centered (Not IT-Centered):** Institutions succeed when they design for struggling students first, not as an afterthought. This is how you close equity gaps and strengthen belonging.
